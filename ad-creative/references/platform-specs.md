# Platform Specs Reference

Complete character limits and format rules for each ad platform. Validate every piece of copy against these before delivery.

---

## Google Ads — Responsive Search Ads (RSAs)

| Element         | Limit            | Quantity  |
|-----------------|------------------|-----------|
| Headline        | 30 characters    | Up to 15  |
| Description     | 90 characters    | Up to 4   |
| Display URL path| 15 chars each    | 2 paths   |

**RSA-specific rules (critical):**
- Headlines are combined and displayed in any order by Google's algorithm
- Every headline must make sense **independently** and **in any combination**
- Pin headlines to specific positions only when absolutely necessary — it reduces Google's ability to optimize
- Include at least:
  - 1 keyword-focused headline (matches search intent)
  - 1 benefit-focused headline (outcome or value prop)
  - 2–3 CTA headlines ("Start Free Trial", "Get a Demo Today")
- Aim for 8–10 headlines minimum; 15 gives Google the most to work with
- Use sentence case, not title case or all caps

**Description rules:**
- Descriptions complement headlines — don't repeat them
- Use for: proof points, objection handling, urgency, CTAs
- At least 2 descriptions required; 4 is ideal

## Meta Ads (Facebook & Instagram)

| Element         | Limit                        | Notes                          |
|-----------------|------------------------------|--------------------------------|
| Primary text    | 125 chars visible (2,200 max)| Front-load the hook            |
| Headline        | 40 chars recommended         | Appears below the image/video  |
| Description     | 30 chars recommended         | Below headline, not always shown|
| URL display link| 40 characters                | Optional                       |

**Meta-specific rules:**
- First 125 characters of primary text are shown without "See More" — make them count
- The hook (first sentence) must stop the scroll; leads with pain, outcome, or curiosity
- Primary text can be longer (up to 2,200 chars) for retargeting audiences who engage more
- Headlines appear below the creative, not above — they support the image, not introduce it
- Descriptions often don't display on mobile — don't rely on them for key information
- Avoid text in images (reduces reach due to platform policy)

## LinkedIn Ads

| Element      | Recommended Limit | Max Limit | Notes                        |
|--------------|-------------------|-----------|------------------------------|
| Intro text   | 150 characters    | 600       | Above the image              |
| Headline     | 70 characters     | 200       | Below the image              |
| Description  | 100 characters    | 300       | Appears in some placements   |

**LinkedIn-specific rules:**
- Intro text is cut off on mobile after ~150 chars — lead with the most important information
- Professional tone works better here than on Meta — this is a work context
- Job titles and company names in copy can increase relevance ("For growth-stage ops teams")
- CTAs tend toward "Download," "Learn More," "Request Demo" rather than urgency plays
- Higher CPCs than other platforms — copy must be very targeted to be cost-effective

## TikTok Ads

| Element       | Recommended | Max  | Notes             |
|---------------|-------------|------|-------------------|
| Ad text       | 80 chars    | 100  | Above the video   |
| Display name  | 40 chars    | 40   | Brand name        |

**TikTok-specific rules:**
- The video carries most of the message — text is secondary
- Ad text should complement, not summarize, the video
- Tone should match TikTok's native, informal style
- Hook must appear in the first 1–3 seconds of video
- On-screen text in the video often performs better than the ad text field

---

## Twitter / X Ads

| Element      | Limit         | Notes               |
|--------------|---------------|---------------------|
| Tweet text   | 280 characters| The main ad copy    |
| Headline     | 70 characters | Card headline       |
| Description  | 200 characters| Card description    |

**Twitter/X-specific rules:**
- All 280 characters are usable if needed, but concise often wins
- Hashtags and mentions count toward character limit
- Conversational tone performs better than overtly promotional
- Cards (image + headline + description) are used for direct response; tweet text alone for engagement

---

## Format Checklist Before Delivery

Before presenting any copy to the user, run through this:

- [ ] Every headline is under its platform's character limit
- [ ] Every description is under its platform's character limit  
- [ ] RSA headlines each make sense independently
- [ ] RSA headlines make sense in any combination with each other
- [ ] At least 2–3 RSA headlines have clear CTAs
- [ ] Meta primary text front-loads the hook in the first 125 chars
- [ ] LinkedIn intro text is under 150 chars
- [ ] Anything over the limit is flagged with a trimmed alternative inline
